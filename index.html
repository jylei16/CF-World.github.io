Are Text-to-Image Models Inductivist Turkeys?
A Counterfactual Benchmark for Causal Reasoning

The "Inductivist Turkey" Dilemma: We argue that current text-to-image models rely on memorized statistical correlations (priors) rather than a genuine understanding of objective world laws.
CF-World Benchmark: We introduce a novel benchmark with a three-level progressive framework (Factual, Explicit, and Implicit Counterfactuals) to rigorously test if models can break free from conventional priors.
CF-Eval Pipeline: We propose an automated evaluation system featuring new metrics like Prior Resistance Rate (PRR) and Reasoning Retention Rate (RRR) to accurately quantify true reasoning capabilities.
Findings & Root Cause: Extensive evaluations show SOTA models fail on counterfactuals due to a lack of logical decoupling. High-dimensional statistical priors force them to rely on language priors (concept lock-in) rather than separating true causal variables from visual attributes.

1. The CF-World Benchmark

To rigorously evaluate underlying reasoning, CF-World employs a three-level progressive logic across 1,091 scientific principles and 3,273 prompts. This design acts as a control variable method to isolate a model's visual rendering ability from its logical reasoning capacity.

2. CF-Eval & Key Results

We introduce CF-Eval, a VLM-based automated evaluation pipeline. To rigorously quantify models' true reasoning capabilities, we propose two novel metrics:

  • Prior Resistance Rate (PRR): Measures a model's ability to overcome entrenched real-world priors when given explicit counterfactual instructions.
  • Reasoning Retention Rate (RRR): Assesses whether models can maintain reasoning-dependent counterfactual generation without explicit visual cues.

Main Evaluation Results

Extensive evaluations reveal a stark reality: even state-of-the-art models experience significant performance degradation when confronted with explicit (L2) and implicit (L3) counterfactuals. Closed-source models currently hold a distinct advantage in mitigating prior entanglement.

3. Why Models Fail: A Decoupling Perspective

Our mechanistic investigation reveals that the degradation stems from a fundamental inability to decouple. Because these models primarily learn pixel co-occurrences, they struggle to separate independent causal variables (logical reasoning) and basic attribute modules (visual recombination).
